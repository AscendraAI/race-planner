How this calculator predicts your time

A HYROX finish is run + stations + roxzone. The official 8 km (8×1 km) is pure running; the Roxzone adds roughly 6.5% on top.

  • Compromised running: in-race runs are 25–45 s/km slower than a fresh 5K (sled & lunge fatigue). We add a level-based offset.
  • Roxzone: 32–50 s per transition × 8. Time you can cut without extra fitness.
  • Stations: distributed by level & gender from real averages.

FAQ

How is my HYROX finish time estimated?
From your all-out 5K pace plus a level-based compromised-run offset across the 8 runs, station times from a lookup built on 400K+ real results, and the ROX-zone transitions.
How do I get faster at HYROX?
The biggest lever is compromised-run pacing — holding run pace after each station. The split view shows exactly where time is lost.
Does it cover gender, division and age?
Yes. Station benchmarks and percentiles adjust by gender, division and age group.
